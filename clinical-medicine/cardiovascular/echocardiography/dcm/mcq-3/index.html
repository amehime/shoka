  1. The LVIDd measures at 6cm. What does the patient have?

    • Normal LV

    • Severely dilated LV

  2. The LVIDs is 5.5cm. What is the fractional shortening?

    • 8%
    • 16%
    • 32%
    • 24%
    • 30%

  1. What is the visual ejection fraction?

    • 45-50%
    • 35-40%
    • 15-20%
    • 20-25%
    • 40-45%

  2. The MAPSE is around:

    • 10mm
    • 5mm
    • 15mm
    • 20mm

  1. This patient has evidence of:
    • Mild eccentric MR
    • Severe functional MR
    • Mild functional MR
    • Moderate functional MR

  1. A similar patient presents a few days later. What is the diagnosis?

    • Moderate MR
    • Severe MR
    • Mild MR
    • Moderate – severe MR

    • Normal diastolic function

    • Moderate diastolic dysfunction
    • Severe diastolic dysfunction
